🐱 Cat Feeding Guide

Feeding your cat correctly is essential for maintaining a healthy weight and preventing obesity or malnutrition. This guide explains how to estimate your cat’s daily food needs using veterinary nutrition principles.

How Much Should I Feed My Cat?

The amount depends on weight, activity level, and food calorie density. Most adult cats require approximately 40–50 calories per kilogram of body weight per day.

Cat Calorie Formula

Veterinarians use this formula:

RER = 70 × (body weight in kg)0.75

This is multiplied by a factor based on life stage (kitten, neutered, weight loss, etc.).

Feeding Tips

  • Feed 2–3 meals per day
  • Always provide fresh water
  • Monitor your cat’s weight regularly
  • Adjust portions based on results

Wet Food vs Dry Food

Dry food contains ~3500–4200 kcal/kg, while wet food contains fewer calories but more moisture. Always check packaging for exact values.
